covid-19 vaccination rollout

WHO, UNICEF, Gavi and partners are working together to help prepare countries to be ready to introduce a COVID-19 vaccine.
Adaptable guidance, tools, trainings, and advocacy materials are being developed to support countries in preparing for COVID-19 vaccination. Please visit this page regularly for updates.

Stay aware of the latest COVID-19 information by regularly checking updates from WHO and your national and local public health authorities. If COVID-19 is spreading in your community, stay safe by taking some simple precautions, such as physical distancing, wearing a mask, keeping rooms well ventilated, avoiding crowds, cleaning your hands, and coughing into a bent elbow or tissue. vaccine 
rollout tracker

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention said on Wednesday, February 3rd, about 27.2 million people have received at least one dose of a Covid-19 vaccine, including about 6.4 million people who have been fully vaccinated.
The work to distribute the vaccine comes at one of the darkest points of the pandemic. More than 448,000 people in the United States have died after contracting the virus.
Federal regulators have given emergency approval to vaccines developed by Pfizer-BioNTech and Moderna. Both vaccines require patients to receive two doses spaced weeks apart.
The federal government has delivered about 55.9 million doses to states, territories and federal agencies.
